## Chapter 16: "The Hunt"

Target Word Count: 1,700 words
POV: Frankie Sullivan, third person
Setting: South Side streets, alleys, abandoned buildings
Time: Week after hotel massacre, various times

**Purpose:** Show Frankie going underground while police pressure increases, but continuing systematic elimination of O'Malley organization.

**Beats:**

- She shoots him twice from behind dumpster, disappears before body discovered. Ghost story spreading: Sullivan girl can't be stopped.

**Dialogue Topics:** Police radio chatter about APB. Kowalski arguing with captain about evidence. Neighborhood whispers about Frankie sightings. Billy's last words (none—shot from behind).

**Emotional Arc:** Frankie physically deteriorating but mentally sharpening. Becoming pure predator, accepting this life. Police net tightening but she's adapted.

**Key Details:** Two rounds fired (thirty remaining). Fifteen total kills. Living in abandoned buildings. No sleep for three days. Billy shot from ambush.
